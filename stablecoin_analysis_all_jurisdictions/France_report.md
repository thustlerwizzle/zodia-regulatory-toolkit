## Regulatory Requirements Summary

### **Summary of Regulatory Research Findings: Cryptocurrency & Digital Assets**

**1. Key Regulatory Requirements**
- **Stablecoin Oversight:** Programs must integrate robust Anti-Money Laundering (AML), Know Your Customer (KYC), and transaction monitoring systems. Issuers are expected to maintain reserves, ensure redemption stability, and provide transparency.
- **Monetary Sovereignty Safeguards:** Regulators, particularly central banks, emphasize frameworks to prevent digital assets from undermining national currency control, capital flow management, and monetary policy.
- **Enterprise-Grade Compliance:** Solutions for institutional adoption (e.g., wallets, on/off-ramps, payment cards) must embed compliance-by-design, aligning with financial services regulations for security and consumer protection.

**2. Compliance Obligations**
- **Risk-Based Onboarding:** Mandatory customer due diligence (CDD) and enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk users, powered increasingly by AI-driven screening and monitoring tools.
- **Transaction Monitoring:** Real-time surveillance of stablecoin transactions for suspicious activity, with reporting obligations to Financial Intelligence Units (FIUs).
- **Partnership Diligence:** Firms (e.g., Dotfile & Bastion) must ensure their integrated compliance stacks meet regulatory standards across all operational jurisdictions.

**3. Recent Regulatory Changes & Trends**
- **Shift in Perception:** Acknowledgment at forums like Davos 2026 that crypto is moving beyond its "illicit money" stigma, prompting more nuanced, activity-based regulation rather than blanket restrictions.
- **Focus on Stablecoins:** Accelerated regulatory clarity for stablecoins globally, treating them as payment systems or e-money, with strict operational and reserve requirements.
- **Cross-Border Coordination:** Increased dialogue among international regulators (e.g., BIS, FSB, IMF) on harmonizing rules to address gaps and prevent regulatory arbitrage.

**4. Jurisdiction-Specific Requirements**
- **United States:** Stablecoin issuers likely subject to state money transmitter licenses, federal AML rules (Bank Secrecy Act), and potential federal legislation for payment stablecoins. SEC may assert securities law over certain programs.
- **European Union:** Compliance with Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), which imposes strict reserve, governance, and licensing requirements for stablecoin issuers.
- **Other Major Jurisdictions:** UK, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Japan are implementing bespoke regimes for stablecoins, often requiring licensing, audits, and adherence to AML/CFT directives.

**5. Enforcement Actions & Guidance**
- **Enforcement Priority:** Regulatory actions focus on unlicensed stablecoin issuance, inadequate AML controls, and deceptive reserve claims. Recent years show increased penalties for compliance failures.
- **Guidance from Davos 2026:** Central bankers signaled that while crypto's role is evolving, regulations will prioritize protecting monetary sovereignty. This may lead to stricter controls on large-scale stablecoin adoption and interoperability with traditional finance.
- **Industry-Led Solutions:** Partnerships like Dotfile-Bastion reflect proactive compliance adoption, anticipating stricter enforcement. Regulators may look favorably on such integrated risk management systems.
