**Requirement:** Stablecoin issuers must be authorized by the Isle of Man Financial Services Authority (IOMFSA) under the Financial Services Act 2008 or Designated Businesses (Registration and Oversight) Act 2015.

**Impact:** Operating without authorization constitutes illegal activity, leading to enforcement actions, fines, license revocation, and reputational damage.

**Recommendation:** Immediately establish a policy requiring formal application to IOMFSA for appropriate authorization before any stablecoin issuance. Policy should define the authorization process, responsible personnel, and documentation requirements.

### Gap 2: GAP-002
**Requirement:** Issuers must maintain adequate reserves (full backing) for issued stablecoins, with assets held in secure, segregated accounts. Regular independent audits of reserves are required.

**Current Status:** No policy exists for reserve management, custody, or audit procedures.

**Gap:** No framework to ensure stablecoins are fully backed by appropriate assets, posing significant risk of insolvency and loss of peg.

**Impact:** Risk of consumer harm, loss of trust, regulatory sanctions for misrepresentation, and potential collapse of the stablecoin if redemptions cannot be met.

**Recommendation:** Develop a comprehensive Reserve Management Policy detailing: 1) Acceptable reserve assets (e.g., cash, high-quality liquid assets), 2) Custody arrangements with regulated custodians, 3) Segregation of client funds, 4) Frequency and scope of independent audits, and 5) public reporting of reserve composition.

### Gap 3: GAP-003
**Requirement:** Robust Anti-Money Laundering (AML), Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF), and sanctions compliance programs are mandatory, including KYC/CDD, transaction monitoring, and reporting suspicious activity.

**Current Status:** No AML/CTF or sanctions compliance policy documented.

**Gap:** Lack of foundational financial crime prevention controls required for all financial services businesses in the Isle of Man.

**Impact:** High risk of facilitating money laundering or terrorist financing, leading to severe regulatory penalties, criminal liability for directors, and loss of correspondent banking relationships.

**Recommendation:** Implement a full AML/CTF/Sanctions Policy aligned with Isle of Man AML/CFT Code and Proceeds of Crime Act 2008.
